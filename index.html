To the woman who occupies my every thought,

Mariam

A confession written in the quiet hours when courage finds its voice

Some encounters happen in daylight, ordinary and expected. Ours happened under the stars, and nothing about it felt ordinary. The night we met, I wasn't prepared for you. I walked into that moment thinking it would be just another evening, and then there you were—and suddenly the world felt different, charged with a possibility I hadn't known I was seeking.

Your beauty struck me first—I won't pretend otherwise. In that dim light, you seemed to glow with something beyond physical presence. The way you moved, the way shadows played across your face when you smiled, the elegance in your gestures. I found myself forgetting what I'd meant to say, my thoughts scattering like light through a prism. You were, and are, absolutely breathtaking.

But beauty, I've learned, is just the beginning. As we talked that night, I discovered something even more captivating—the woman behind that beautiful face. Your laugh, rich and genuine. The intelligence in your eyes when you spoke about things that matter to you. The way you listened, really listened, as if my words carried weight. You possess a rare combination of grace and depth that makes it impossible not to want more.

Since that night, you've occupied a space in my mind I didn't know was vacant. I catch myself replaying our conversation—the sound of your voice, the way you tilted your head when considering something I'd said, how time seemed to slow down when we locked eyes. I've been trying to find the right words to tell you this, the right moment, but I've realized there's no perfect time for truth. There's only now, and this need to let you know what you've awakened in me.

I'm drawn to you, Mariam, in ways that surprise me. You make me want to be better—more articulate, more present, more worthy of your time and attention. I want to know the stories you haven't told anyone, the dreams you chase in quiet moments, the things that make your heart race. I want to discover what makes you laugh so hard you can't breathe, what music moves you, what keeps you up at night thinking.

This might be bold—showing up like this, putting my feelings into words you can't unhear. But you deserve honesty, not games or half-truths. So here it is: I would love the chance to take you out, to see you again in a way that's deliberate and unhurried. To learn who you are beyond that enchanted first meeting. To see if this connection I feel—this pull toward you—is something you recognize too. If there's even a chance you might say yes, it would make this vulnerability worthwhile.

"In all the world, there is no heart for me like yours. In all the world, there is no love for you like mine."

— Maya Angelou

With all the sincerity my heart can muster,

Edwin
